Frequently Answered Queries

Everything regarding the bereavement mutual support system explained plainly.

Does this scheme cover relatives visiting from Zambia?

No, our Twende Zambia UK bereavement scheme strictly covers registered members and their dependents who are official, active residents living in the United Kingdom. It is designed to aid diaspora residents regarding foreign repatriation complications back to Zambia.

How exactly is the £10,000 repatriation money raised and stored?

Our platform functions as an active diaspora crowdfunding/mutual aid network. Once our target list of 335 members is reached, each member's commit of £30 generates a pool of £10,000. These funds are held in a secure UK bank account, regulated by community trustees, and released within 48-72 hours of verified bereavement reports.

What happens if an active member fails to pay their £30 contribution?

Solidarity relies on absolute commitment. If a member fails to fulfill their £30 bereavement contribution within the designated 14-day grace window, their membership status defaults to inactive. Inactive members cannot benefit from the £10,000 payout coverage until outstanding dues are paid, and a penalty reinstatement fee is cleared.

Why is there a strict 6-month maturity waiting rule for the £10,000 payout?

To prevent fraud and maintain financial balance, new registered members must maintain their dues for 6 months before being eligible for the full £10,000 payout. If bereavement happens within this initial 6-month maturity interval, the family receives compassionate mental support, but is not eligible for the total cash repatriation benefit.
